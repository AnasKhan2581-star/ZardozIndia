What is Zardozi Hand embroidery?

Zardozi/Zardosi is a type of traditional hand embroidery done with material made of copper on raw silk, crepe, satin, georgette, shiffon, net or velvet fabric base. Designs are often created using dabka(french wire), different coloured threads, zircons and can incorporate pearls, beads, and precious stones. Historically, it was used to adorn the walls of royal tents, scabbards, wall hangings, and the paraphernalia of regal elephants and horses.Later, it was used to make bridal dresses. Gold embroidery has existed in India since the time of the Rigveda, between 1500 and 1200 BC. It prospered during the 17th century during the reign of the Mughal emperor Akbar, but later a loss of royal patronage and industrialization led to its decline. The craft began to experience a resurgence in popularity following India's independence in 1947. Although Zardozi is an International Handwork as it is famous in the USA, UK, Canada, Iran, Bangladesh, etc. but it's very popular in Mumbai, Delhi, Punjab, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal etc. Very expensive premium brands like Sabyasachi lehenga, Manish Malhotra lehenga, ritukumar dresses and lehenga often have zardozi/zardosi work.
